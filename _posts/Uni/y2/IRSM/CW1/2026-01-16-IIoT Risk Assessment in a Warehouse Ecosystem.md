This assessment covers the 150,000m2 warehouse’s IIoT ecosystem, specifically the seven asset
classes (AN1–AP4) Table 1 bridging the IT/OT network. The analysis is limited to deliberate
cyberattacks and exploitation of technical vulnerabilities (CVEs) using the STRIDE and ISO
27005:2024 frameworks to calculate risk via reproducibility, business value Table 2, and CIA
impact Table 4. It was decided to use bespoke formula to derive risk over DREAD as it allows
for better structure in the current context. For the Risk Register, a qualitative reproducibility
metric was selected over the EPSS model, as this frequency estimation framework would be
too narrowly focused, undermining the report’s goal on supporting continued use of all listed IIoT
devices in the current environment. Explicitly excluded are environmental hazards, accidental
failures, and social engineering, ensuring a strictly technical focus on the digital ”blast radius” of
the deployed systems.

This is a coursework that was submitted as part of a Information Risk and Security Management module from Year 2 as a part of the Cyber Security Degree at University of Warwick
